# Personas

## Persona 1: Agency Operator Owen
- Role: Founder or operations lead at a small agency with 2 to 10 people.
- Environment: Lives in Discord for internal coordination, client updates, and quick handoffs.
- Trigger: A client-facing or internal follow-up step gets missed because it depended on somebody remembering to post an update.
- Pain: Work is technically happening, but the operating picture is fuzzy and follow-through is unreliable.
- Current Workaround: Spreadsheets for status, Discord messages for nudges, and manual copy-paste updates when something changes.
- Desired Outcome: Know that a recurring workflow fired, completed, or failed without chasing the team.
- Buying Signal: Already pays for specialized tools when they clearly reduce coordination overhead.
- Objection: Will not tolerate a setup process that feels heavier than the manual workaround.

## Persona 2: Service Business Manager Maya
- Role: Manager running delivery or fulfillment for a small service business.
- Environment: Uses chat plus lightweight SaaS tools, but does not have internal engineering support.
- Trigger: Customer trust drops when a repeated operational step is late, invisible, or inconsistent.
- Pain: Repeated status-checking steals time from actual delivery, and manual oversight does not scale.
- Current Workaround: Shared docs, inbox flags, calendar reminders, and ad hoc checklist reviews.
- Desired Outcome: A simple way to confirm that key operational steps happened and surface exceptions fast.
- Buying Signal: The pain touches revenue, renewals, or team utilization.
- Objection: Does not want a broad operations suite or a long implementation project.

## Who We Are Not Serving Yet
- Teams primarily asking for enterprise reporting, permissions matrices, or deep compliance workflows.
- Buyers looking for a generic chat replacement.
- Organizations that need multi-system orchestration before they care about one painful workflow being solved well.

## Shared Traits Of Good Early Customers
- They already feel the pain weekly.
- They can describe the current workaround in detail.
- They use chat as part of the real workflow, not just for casual communication.
- They are willing to trade breadth for speed, clarity, and dependability.
