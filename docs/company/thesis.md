# Company Thesis

## Mission
Help time-starved operators turn fragile manual follow-up into dependable, visible workflows that run through the tools they already use.

## Initial Market
- Solo founders and very small teams running client delivery, internal operations, or recurring business processes without custom internal software.
- Teams that already coordinate through chat tools such as Discord or Slack and are feeling the limits of reminders, spreadsheets, and copy-paste handoffs.

## Core Pain
These operators lose time and trust because important follow-up steps live in people's heads or in brittle manual systems. Work gets delayed, status becomes unclear, and nobody is confident that a recurring process actually happened.

## Current Workaround
- Spreadsheets used as lightweight workflow trackers.
- Chat messages or reminders used as informal triggers.
- Manual status checks and copy-paste updates across tools.

## Product Thesis
If we make one recurring operational workflow easy to trigger, confirm, and monitor from a familiar chat-based environment, small operators will adopt it faster than a broader all-in-one operations platform.

## Initial Wedge
Start with webhook-backed workflow alerts and confirmations for operators who already use Discord as part of their day-to-day operating loop.

This wedge is attractive because:
- the pain is frequent and easy to observe,
- the first version can be mocked and validated quickly,
- the value can be demonstrated before building a large automation surface.

## Ideal Customer Profile
- Runs a business or team with repeated operational steps.
- Already uses chat as a coordination layer.
- Has enough workflow volume that missed steps cost time, money, or customer confidence.
- Will pay for reduced mental overhead and improved execution confidence.

## Why This Wins
- It meets users inside an existing habit instead of asking them to adopt a new platform first.
- It solves a sharp operational pain before expanding into broader workflow management.
- It can be sold on speed, clarity, and trust rather than on an oversized feature list.

## Non-Goals
- Do not build a generic team chat platform.
- Do not build enterprise workflow software before the narrow wedge proves demand.
- Do not expand into multiple channels until one chat-centric use case shows repeated activation and retention.

## 12-Month Success Definition
- A focused product used weekly by a repeatable customer segment.
- Evidence that customers trust the workflow enough to rely on it for real operational steps.
- A measurable link between product use and reduced manual follow-up.
- A feedback-to-launch operating loop that can support continuous shipping without adding team-scale overhead.

## Strategic Bets
- Chat-adjacent workflow tooling is easier to validate than a full operations suite.
- Mocked-first releases will help prove demand before expensive integration work.
- Activation and repeated workflow completion matter more than breadth of features.

## Kill Criteria
Revisit the thesis if any of the following remain true after repeated validation attempts:
- Users do not treat the workflow as important enough to configure.
- Setup friction is consistently higher than perceived value.
- Customers want broader reporting or automation before they care about the core alerting workflow.
